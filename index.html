Projects
Featured · FSAE Electric

Liquid Cooling Loop — 26E Racecar

Led analysis, design, and testing of a liquid cooling loop for a Formula SAE Electric racecar, managing thermal requirements for the motor and inverter while working within FSAE rules constraints and tight packaging limitations. Built a MATLAB simulation workflow to predict coolant temperatures across a dynamic race lap, calculated pressure drops using the Swamee-Jain equation, and collaborated with the EE team on a 97.4% efficient 21V boost converter to hit our 8 LpM flow target. Designed the full cooling architecture in SolidWorks including 3D-printed pump mounts and sheet metal tabs, instrumented the system with thermocouples and a Reed Switch flow meter, and implemented embedded fan control on Arduino with custom PWM curves across six 36W fans to reduce power draw while maintaining optimal temperatures.
