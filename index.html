About

Third year ME student at UCR

I'm interested in thermal systems and energy optimization — particularly where simulation meets design and hands-on problem solving.

At Highlander Racing, I lead the cooling subteam, developing MATLAB thermal simulations for our FSAE Electric vehicle's liquid cooling loop. Scaled the team from 2 → 11 members, standardized documentation, and integrated new sensor suite.

Last summer I interned at Utthunga in Houston and Bengaluru — traveling to India for the first half of the internship. I was on the Asset Performance Management team, where the broader work centered on building digital twins and predictive models for global industrial clients. My work spanned thermal simulation, Python tooling, and hands-on exposure to DCS and APM systems — including FMEA sessions, plant walkthroughs, and structured learning across energy management and process engineering tools like ASPEN.

Projects
Featured · FSAE Electric

Liquid Cooling Loop — 26E Racecar

Led analysis, design, and testing of a liquid cooling loop for a Formula SAE Electric racecar, managing thermal requirements for the motor and inverter while working within FSAE rules constraints and tight packaging limitations. Built a MATLAB simulation workflow to predict coolant temperatures across a dynamic race lap, calculated pressure drops using the Swamee-Jain equation, and collaborated with the EE team on implementing a custom 97.4% efficient 21V boost converter to hit our 8 LpM flow target. Instrumented the system with thermocouples and a Reed Switch flow meter to validate analysis. Enforced strict documentation standards with the standardization of FMEA, engineering drawings, and script documentation. Currently in the process of developing test methods to characterize loop performance with wind tunnel testing.
